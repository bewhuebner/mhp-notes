Far from being unthinking advocates of imperial might, several Roman writers were the most powerful critics of imperialism there have ever been. ‘They create desolation and call it peace’ is a slogan that has often summed up the consequences of military conquest. It was written in the 2nd century CE by the Roman historian [[Tacitus\|Tacitus]], referring to Roman power in Britain.
