The secret, [[Polybius\|Polybius]] suggested, lay in a delicate relationship of [[checks and balances\|checks and balances]] between consuls, the senate and the people, so that neither monarchy nor aristocracy nor democracy ever entirely prevailed. It was, Polybius argued, such balances across the political system that produced the internal stability on which Roman external success was built.
